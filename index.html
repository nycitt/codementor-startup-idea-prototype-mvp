Turn Your Idea to Working Prototype in  Weeks with a Live Mentor

This is a 3-week series of 4 online classes where you’ll learn how to build your startup idea into a working prototype.

Who is this class for?

This class is designed for aspiring entrepreneurs with basic knowledge JavaScript, who are looking to build their own startups.

What will you learn?

This class will touch on the technical aspects of building a working prototype, while also delving into business development, project management, finance and marketing. At the end of the class, you and your new pair-programming buddy will have a live prototype. Who knows, maybe it can even turn into a real business!




Codementor 3-Week MVP Class: Syllabus

Lesson 1

WED 16 SEP

Introduction

Intro: The Trajectory of a Typical Startup (30m)

  • The different stages of a startup
    • Inception
    • Wireframe prototype
    • Build prototype
    • Business plan + show prototype to friends & potential clients
    • Seed capital from friends & small investors
    • Iterate on prototype
    • Investment pipeline: Angel, series A...E, IPO
  • Why it's important to code something you care about
  • Why most startups fail, and why it's not a bad thing

Hands on exercise: brainstorm ideas for your startup (30m)

  • Pick an idea for your startup--it could even be one you have already
  • Explain the importance of having a business partner
  • Discuss the feasibility of each project and have students pair off on the best ones
  • Understanding deadlines: prioritize what can be completed in the course of two weeks

Wireframing time (30m)

  • Vote on the best startup idea, and use that as wireframe guide
  • Learn a few tips and tricks in Balsamiq

Students wireframe (30m)

Students will have an opportunity to build their own wireframes, and will have the opportunity to ask questions to instructor

Homework

You and your coding partner should work on your app and wireframe until the next lesson. The best wireframe will be used as an example in week 2.

Lesson 2

SAT 19 SEP

Dive into Backbone

Getting Started with Code (1.5h)

  • Get students started on Node + Backbone + Parse + Github
  • Properly setting up your dev environment and why it is so important

Coding Time (30m)

Students will get started coding their projects

Homework

Work with your partner on coding the first iteration of your prototype!

Lesson 3

WED 23 SEP

Asana - Project Management

Code Review (30m)

Organizing In Asana (1h)

Why it's so important to keep organized and how you can use Asana to organize your project roadmap and business.

Back to Coding (30m)

Homework

Create a roadmap with your partner for what your roadmap will be and continue working on coding

Lesson 4

WED 23 SEP

Deeper Dive into Backbone

Backbone.js Tips and Tricks (1h)

Strategies of using Backbone effectively

Debugging time (1h)

  • Explain debugging strategies with chrome debugger
  • Help debug a few students' applications as they work together on it

Homework

Work with your partner on refactoring your application using what we learned in class

Lesson 5

WED 23 SEP

Organizing Your Code

Code Review (30m)

Webpack (30m)

How to organize code using Require and Webpack.

Refactor Time (1h)

Homework:

Work with your partner to refactor your code base

Lesson 6

SAT 26 SEP

Where to Go From Here

Review everyone's final code (30m)

Focus on what you want (45m)

Students pick what they are most interested in learning about

Next steps (30m)

  • How to take your startup forward: next steps
  • Lessons I learned
    • Doing startups because they're fun, not for money
    • Knowing when to give up and why it's ok
    • How adjusting to the market ("pivoting") will save your startup

Final Questions (15m)

Frequently Asked Questions

How is this different from other courses?

For only $99, you’ll get to learn hands-on programming skills live with a real online mentor. This includes 4 live classes with instant feedback and hands-on exercises. Having live interaction with a mentor makes learning much easier than watching recorded video tutorials.

What is the class structure?

We have 2 live sessions per week for 4 weeks. Each session is 2 hours long, and will include live lectures, hands-on exercises, and Q&A. You will get to code together with the instructor and other members of the class!

What time are the live classes?

The live classes will be held online at 6PM PDT (GMT-7) / 9PM EDT (GMT-4) on Wednesdays and 11AM PDT (GMT-7) / 2PM EDT (GMT-4) on Saturdays starting from Sept 16th All classes will be recorded. If you'd like to enroll but strongly prefer a different time, please email us.

What do I need to know before the class?

1) Basic Javascript: We will be covering the basics of Backbone.js, Node.js, GitHub, Balsamiq, Require, Webpack, and Parse. Checking out these tools beforehand is recommended but not required. If you're confused it's ok! 2) Startup Idea: come with some ideas of what you would like to build in the next 2 weeks. It could be a startup idea that you're already working on. It could be a totally new tool that will make your life easier. If you're working on something you care about (vs something we assign), you'll learn a LOT quicker.

What if I miss a class?

All classes will be recorded and you‘ll have access to the recordings at any time. You can also ask questions in our Slack channel and get help from other members of the community.
